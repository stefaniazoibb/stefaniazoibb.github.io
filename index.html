01 — About

From psychology to the physics of vision.

I'm a PhD researcher working where neuroscience, machine learning, and optics meet. My doctoral project, BE-LIGHT, develops light-based technologies and ML models to improve biomedical diagnosis — with a focus on the optical decomposition of the retina as a window into neural function.

Much of my modeling is probabilistic and Bayesian. I lean on Gaussian processes — nonparametric priors over functions — to model noisy, high-dimensional signals with calibrated uncertainty rather than a single point estimate. That framing runs through my work: GP models of natural images and video, GP regression on neural and physiological recordings, and Bayesian neural models that quantify how confident a prediction is, not just what it is. When you're building a diagnostic, that uncertainty is the point.

My path is deliberately interdisciplinary. I started in psychology and neuropsychology in Greece, moved into biomedical engineering in Paris, and now work on the computational and mathematical side of neuroscience — probabilistic modeling, deep learning, and connectionist theory. Along the way I've built Bayesian neural models of pilots' physiological states, run fMRI and 7T studies of arousal, and co-authored work published in Scientific Reports.

I'm drawn to problems where careful modeling meets real signals — a kernel, a posterior, a recording — and reveals how a biological or artificial system actually learns to see.

02 — Research

What I work on.

📈

Gaussian Processes

Nonparametric, kernel-based priors over functions. I use GPs to model natural images, video, and neural signals — choosing kernels that encode spatiotemporal structure and reading the posterior for principled, uncertainty-aware predictions.

📊

Bayesian Inference

Modeling from priors and likelihoods to posteriors: Bayesian neural models, probabilistic regression, and calibrated uncertainty on noisy, multi-modal data — so a prediction comes with how much to trust it.

👁️

The Retina, Decomposed

Machine-learning and computational models for the optical decomposition of the retina — understanding how retinal circuits transform incoming light into neural signal.

💡

Light-Based Diagnostics

Developing photonic, light-based technologies for biomedical diagnosis within the European BE-LIGHT interdisciplinary network.

🧠

Neural Coding & Vision

Spike-triggered analysis, receptive-field estimation, and self-supervised representation learning — how biological and artificial visual systems build the features they use to see.

🌀

Dynamics & Time Series

Nonlinear dynamical models of excitability, bifurcation analysis, and ordinal-pattern methods for characterizing complex neural time series.

Shape vs. Texture in Self-Supervised Vision

Probing self-supervised (CLAPP) vision encoders trained under different schedules to measure whether learned features rely on shape or texture cues, using linear probes on frozen representations and cue-conflict stimuli.

Gaussian Processes on Natural Images & Video

Kernel-based probabilistic modeling of natural scenes: constructing GP priors whose covariance encodes spatial and temporal structure, conditioning on data to obtain the posterior, and tuning kernel hyperparameters by marginal likelihood. Includes visualizations of samples morphing from prior to data-conditioned posterior, with uncertainty bands throughout.

Bayesian Neural Model of Operator State

At Thales AVS: a Bayesian neural model forecasting how quickly pilots return to physiological baseline, fusing eye-tracking, ECG, respiration and accelerometer signals recorded during flight simulation. The Bayesian treatment yields predictions with calibrated uncertainty — essential when the output informs safety-critical decisions.
